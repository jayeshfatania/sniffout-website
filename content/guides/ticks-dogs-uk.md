Ticks are small parasitic arachnids that attach to your dog's skin and feed on blood. In the UK, tick season runs roughly from March through to October, with peak activity in spring and early autumn. Woodland, heathland, and long grass are the highest-risk environments.

## Where to check after a walk

After any walk through woodland or long grass, run your hands through your dog's coat and check the following areas carefully:

- Around the ears and inside the ear flap
- Between the toes and under the paws
- Around the collar area and neck
- Under the armpits and groin
- Around the base of the tail

Ticks are often small enough to mistake for a skin tag or small bump. They will feel firm and slightly raised.

## Lungworm and tick prevention

If your dog is not already on a regular tick prevention treatment, it is worth a conversation with your vet. Several spot-on and chewable treatments are available that provide effective protection. Standard worming products do not cover ticks or lungworm.
