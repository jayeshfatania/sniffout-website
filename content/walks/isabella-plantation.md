description_long: |
  A short loop inside Richmond Park but with a completely different character. The Isabella Plantation is a woodland garden - dense with mature oak and sweet chestnut, and underplanted with azaleas and rhododendrons that make the place almost unrecognisable in April and May when they come into flower. Dogs must be kept on lead throughout, as in the rest of the park.

  The paths wind past several small streams and ponds where dogs can drink. The ground is shaded and the paths mulched or gravelled - good underfoot even when the rest of the park is dry. At just 1.4km it is a short walk, but the density of smell and the variety of plants make it richly interesting for a dog at a pace they set.

  This is one of the quieter corners of Richmond Park. Weekday mornings are particularly calm. Worth combining with the wider deer loop for a full outing, or visiting alone when you want something gentle and contained.
