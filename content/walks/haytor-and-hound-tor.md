description_long: |
  Two of Dartmoor's most recognisable granite tors in one route, with open moorland connecting them. Haytor is the more famous, rising clearly above the surrounding heath with a rock face that climbers use. Hound Tor is wilder and less visited, with a collapsed medieval farmstead at its base. The walk between them crosses open moor with long views in all directions.

  The terrain is rough moorland - a mix of short grass, heather, and rocky ground. Good boots are essential. After significant rain, lower sections can be very boggy; the higher ground drains better but is uneven underfoot. Dartmoor ponies graze freely across the moor and will not panic at a dog, but keeping close control near them is the right approach.

  This is open-access land and dogs can be off lead across most of it. The moorland character makes for an excellent day out for a dog that wants real space and genuine countryside. Cold, wet, and windswept in bad weather - and spectacular in good. Come prepared for either.
