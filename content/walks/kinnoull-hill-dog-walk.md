Kinnoull Hill rises above Perth on the east bank of the Tay, a ridge of beech and oak woodland designated Scotland's first Woodland Park in 1991. The walk to Kinnoull Tower takes under an hour from the Jubilee car park, and the view from the top over the broad arc of the River Tay, with the Ochil Hills and Strathearn visible on a clear day, is consistently regarded as one of the finest accessible viewpoints in the country for the effort involved.

The cliff on the south face drops approximately 150 metres, partly screened by mature woodland. It is easy to be closer to the edge than you realise. Dogs must be on leads for the entire section near and around Kinnoull Tower. Away from the cliff, the woodland is excellent off-lead territory.

Red squirrels and roe deer are both present. Autumn is an outstanding season here, with beech and oak turning gold through the canopy. Paths can be boggy on lower sections after prolonged rain, and icy near the cliff in cold spells.

Both car parks are free. No facilities on site. Willowgate Cafe in Perth has riverside outdoor seating on the Tay, about 1.5 miles away, and welcomes dogs outside.

## Frequently asked questions

<div itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">

### Is the cliff near Kinnoull Tower genuinely dangerous?

<div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
<div itemprop="text">

Yes. The south face of Kinnoull Hill drops approximately 150 metres. Kinnoull Tower sits at the very edge and the path passes close to unfenced cliff sections. The drop is partly screened by trees, which can create a false sense of security. Dogs must be kept on leads for the entire cliff-edge section near and around the tower.

<div itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">

### Is Kinnoull Hill genuinely one of Scotland's best viewpoints?

<div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
<div itemprop="text">

It is widely regarded as one of the best accessible urban viewpoints in Scotland. The view from the tower over the broad meandering arc of the River Tay, with the Ochil Hills and Strathearn visible on clear days, is exceptional for a viewpoint requiring so little effort to reach. VisitScotland promotes it as a Perthshire highlight.

<div itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">

### Is there water access for dogs at Kinnoull Hill?

<div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
<div itemprop="text">

No river or loch access on the main circuit. The River Tay is visible far below the cliffs but inaccessible from the hill path. Dogs can access the Tay on the Perth North Inch and South Inch riverside paths in the city, which connect to the Kinnoull area via Bridgend.

### Who manages Kinnoull Hill and is there a charge to visit?

<div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
<div itemprop="text">

Jointly managed by Forestry and Land Scotland and Perth and Kinross Council. Designated Scotland's first Woodland Park in 1991. No entry fee. Both car parks are currently free. Scottish Outdoor Access Code applies.
