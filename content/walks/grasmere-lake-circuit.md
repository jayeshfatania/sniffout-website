description_long: |
  Grasmere is a small lake by Lake District standards but the circuit around it is one of the most reliably satisfying walks in the area. The route stays close to the water for much of its length, with views across the lake to the surrounding fells - Helm Crag and the Dunmail Raise ridge visible to the north, the lower slopes of Loughrigg to the south.

  The terrain is mixed: tarmac and gravel near the village, softer lakeside paths through the meadows, and rougher ground on the south side of the circuit. The lower paths can be muddy in winter and spring, when the Lake District's rainfall shows itself most clearly. Waterproof boots are worthwhile for most of the year.

  Dogs can be off lead on much of the circuit, with care near the village and the more popular lakeside sections. Grasmere village has a cafe, toilets, and the famous gingerbread shop. The route passes the churchyard where Wordsworth is buried. This is a short walk by Lake District standards but gives a proper feel of the landscape without requiring a full fell day.
