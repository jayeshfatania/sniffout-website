# Fact Check: Cockapoo Walking Guide

## 1. Overall Assessment

**PASS WITH ONE MINOR CORRECTION**

13 of 14 checks verified. One spelling error requiring correction (Check 13, "high-matt" should be "high-mat"). No factual errors. No brachycephalic data present. No prohibited language. No em dashes. All walk distances accurate. The guide is factually sound and ready for Editor review after the spelling correction is applied.

---

## 2. Checks

---

**Check 1 - "60 minutes of physical activity per day, split across at least two walks" (Waggel UK; Animalife; Your Family Vets Knaresborough)**

**VERIFIED**

Research confirms near-universal agreement: Waggel UK states "approximately 60 minutes total daily," Animalife states "one hour of exercise per day," Your Family Vets Knaresborough states "around 60 minutes of exercise daily." All three cited sources agree. The two-walk minimum is consistent with all sources reviewed. Attribution is accurate.

---

**Check 2 - Three size categories from Cockapoo Club of Great Britain (Toy up to 5.4 kg, Miniature 5.4-9.1 kg, Standard 9.1-13.6 kg)**

**VERIFIED**

The Cockapoo Club of Great Britain does recognise three main sizes. The specific weight figures in the guide (Toy up to 5.4 kg; Miniature 5.4-9.1 kg; Standard 9.1-13.6 kg) come from Waggel UK, which the guide correctly attributes. The guide states "The Cockapoo Club of Great Britain recognises three sizes" and then attributes the weight figures to "Waggel UK" - this framing is accurate. The research notes the CCGB acknowledges three sizes while the specific weight ranges are sourced from Waggel UK.

Note: the research also records a lower floor of 2.7 kg for the Toy category ("Toy: 2.7-5.4 kg"), which the guide simplifies to "up to around 5.4 kg." This simplification is acceptable for a consumer article.

---

**Check 3 - Exercise by size (Toy 20-30 min, Miniature 30-45 min, Standard 60 min minimum)**

**VERIFIED**

Research confirms these figures with strong source agreement: Toy 20-30 minutes, Miniature 30-45 minutes, Standard minimum 60 minutes. Attributed to Waggel UK in the guide - correct, as these are the figures from Waggel UK with corroboration from Doodle Doods and Happytail Puppies. Attribution is accurate.

---

**Check 4 - "3 to 5 miles daily" and "up to 10 miles" ceiling (Waggel UK; Wamiz UK)**

**VERIFIED WITH NOTE**

Wamiz UK states "3-5 miles (5-8 km) comfortably" for healthy adults. Both Waggel UK and Wamiz UK cite "up to 10 miles" as a ceiling, consistently framed as exceptional rather than routine. The guide specifies "a healthy Miniature or Standard adult" - this qualification is important and accurate, as the research notes the 2-mile lower floor from Waggel applies to all sizes including Toy, whereas 3-5 miles is the more commonly cited range for Miniature and Standard specifically.

Note: Waggel UK's lower floor is 2 miles (not 3 miles) when all size variants are included. The guide's 3-mile floor is accurate for Miniature and Standard adults as specified. The qualifier "Miniature or Standard" makes the claim defensible.

Metric conversion: 3-5 miles = approximately 4.8-8.0 km. The guide states "5 to 8 km" - a minor rounding imprecision on the lower end (4.8 km rounded to 5 km) but acceptable for a consumer article.

**Check 5 - Five-minutes-per-month-of-age puppy rule**

**VERIFIED**

Research confirms: "The five-minutes-per-month-of-age rule applies universally and is confirmed by all sources consulted." Cited in the research to Joii Pet Care and confirmed across multiple UK sources. The guide's specific example ("A four-month-old gets no more than 20 minutes per session, twice daily") is mathematically correct (4 months x 5 minutes = 20 minutes). Standard UK veterinary guidance.

The guide also correctly extends this to all vigorous exercise ("garden zoomies, jumping off furniture, and extended rough-and-tumble all count, not only structured walks") - this is confirmed in the research from Joii Pet Care. **Check 9 - CRoW Act lead requirement 1 March to 31 July on open access land**

**VERIFIED**

The guide states: "Under the CRoW Act, dogs on open access land must be kept on a short lead between 1 March and 31 July."

This is consistent with the research file and confirmed across multiple prior Sniffout research documents. The CRoW Act (Countryside and Rights of Way Act 2000) mandatory lead period on open access land is 1 March to 31 July. The guide also correctly lists "Countryside and Rights of Way Act 2000" in the sources footnote.

**Check 10 - Luxating patella 2.7% and hip dysplasia 7.4% from OFA data (Cockapoo Crazy; ManyPets)**

**VERIFIED**

Research confirms: "OFA (Orthopedic Foundation for Animals) data cited by Cockapoo Crazy: 2.7% of tested Cockapoos show luxating patella" and "OFA data (cited by Cockapoo Crazy and ManyPets): 7.4% of screened Cockapoos show hip dysplasia on X-ray."

The guide cites both figures and attributes them to "Cockapoo Crazy; ManyPets" - this matches the research attribution. The research includes the important caveat that these figures "likely undercount true prevalence as only health-tested dogs are in the database." The guide includes this caveat: "these figures come from voluntarily tested dogs and likely undercount the true rate." The caveat is present and accurate.

## 4. Suggestions

**Suggestion 1 - Waggel UK distance lower floor (not a correction, editorial note):**

Waggel UK's published daily distance range starts at 2 miles for all Cockapoos, not 3 miles. The guide's 3-mile lower floor is justified by specifying "Miniature or Standard adult," and the research supports this distinction. The guide is not wrong, but Tom may wish to add a parenthetical noting that Toy Cockapoos sit at the lower end of this range (consistent with what he writes about Toy exercise duration in the size section earlier in the article). This would close the minor gap.

**Suggestion 2 - OFA caveat placement:**

The caveat that OFA figures undercount prevalence (because only voluntarily tested dogs are included) is present and accurate. It could be slightly more prominent - currently it appears in parentheses mid-sentence. This is an editorial call, not a factual concern.

**Suggestion 3 - Cooling technique nuance:**

The guide advises on heat: "apply cool water to the belly and paws - not a full coat drench. A wet dense coat can trap heat rather than release it."

The heat guide research (docs/research/heat-guide-research-april-3.md) completed today establishes a nuance: the "trapping heat" concern applies to static wet towels left on the dog, not to active pouring combined with air movement. The RVC protocol involves soaking the whole body with air movement. The Cockapoo guide's advice is more conservative than the RVC protocol and leans toward the "belly and paws only" approach. This is not factually wrong as a precautionary consumer recommendation, but it is at some variance from the RVC "cool first, transport second" full-body protocol. Tom or the Editor should decide whether to align with the RVC protocol in a future revision.
