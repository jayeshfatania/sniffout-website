## Document 1: French Bulldog Walking Guide

**Overall assessment: PASS WITH MINOR NOTES**

No factual errors requiring correction before publication. One rounding note on the BOAS odds ratio. All veterinary claims and walking guidance verified against sources.

---

### Check 1 - Exercise duration: 30 to 60 minutes per day for a healthy adult, split across two walks

**VERIFIED**

PitPat and Your Family Vets Harrogate both cited in the guide. The 30-60 minute range and two-walk split is consistent with UK vet consensus guidance for the breed. Sources are cited inline.

---

### Check 2 - Puppy exercise rule: five minutes per month of age, applied conservatively on warm days

**VERIFIED**

Standard UK veterinary guidance for large and medium breeds. The guide applies it conservatively to French Bulldogs, which is appropriate given growth plate and joint concerns. The 12-month growth plate maturity claim is consistent with the breed.

---

### Check 3 - Senior dogs from around 7 to 8 years

**VERIFIED**

The guide cites Animalife. The 7-to-8 year senior threshold is consistent with the research file and with general UK vet guidance for medium-sized breeds.

---

### Check 4 - BOAS odds ratio: "approximately 31 times more likely"

**VERIFIED WITH NOTE**

The guide states French Bulldogs are "approximately 31 times more likely to be recorded with a BOAS diagnosis than other dogs" and cites O'Neill et al., Canine Medicine and Genetics, 2021 (PMC8675495).

The research file records the actual OR as 30.89. Rounding to "approximately 31 times" is acceptable and not misleading.

Note for consistency: the methodology page uses the figure of 6.5 times for HRI risk (rounded from 6.49). Both documents round to one decimal or nearest whole number. No correction required, but editors should be aware these are rounded figures if exact numbers are ever needed elsewhere.

---

### Check 5 - Heat-related illness odds ratio: "6.49 times the odds of heat-related illness compared to Labrador Retrievers"

**VERIFIED**

The guide cites O'Neill et al., Scientific Reports, 2020, PMC7303136. The research file confirms OR 6.49 from this study. The figure is reported precisely (not rounded), which is appropriate for an inline citation.

---

### Check 6 - Temperature thresholds: 15C caution, 20C early morning/evening only, 25C do not walk

**VERIFIED**

These thresholds are consistent with the breed-specific guidance reviewed in the canine weather sensitivity research. The 15C lower caution threshold reflects the brachycephalic adjustment appropriate for this breed. PDSA and Kennel Club are cited. No correction required.

---

### Check 7 - Cooling protocol: cool water over neck, armpits, and groin; do not use ice or ice-cold water

**VERIFIED**

Standard UK first-response guidance for canine heatstroke. Consistent with PDSA and VetCompass guidance. The warning against ice-cold water (shock risk) is clinically accurate. PDSA and Kennel Club cited.

---

### Check 8 - Chondrodystrophic claim and spinal/joint predisposition

**VERIFIED**

The guide states French Bulldogs are chondrodystrophic and have a breed-level predisposition to spinal and joint problems. This is correct - French Bulldogs carry the FGF4 retrogene variant associated with chondrodystrophy (shared with Dachshunds, Basset Hounds, and similar breeds). PDSA and Fitzpatrick Referrals cited. No correction required.

---

### Check 9 - Swimming risk: French Bulldogs cannot naturally swim; heavy head and broad chest

**VERIFIED**

The biomechanical explanation (front-heavy build causing head submersion during paddling) is accurate and consistent with the research file. Pets4Homes UK cited. The practical guidance - on-lead near water, shallow paddling only within arm's reach, life jacket for off-lead near open water - is appropriate and consistent with breed-specific safety guidance.

---

### Check 10 - Walk assessments: Isabella Plantation, Burley, Grasmere, Wimbledon, Frensham, Box Hill

**VERIFIED**

Walk assessments are consistent with the French Bulldog research file. Specific details verified:
- Isabella Plantation 1.4 km: matches research file (1.4 km circuit, enclosed woodland, on-lead)
- Frensham: open heathland concern flagged correctly; Great Pond drowning risk flagged correctly
- Box Hill: lower woodland paths assessed separately from the steep zigzag - consistent with research
- Seven Sisters 8.4 km: distance and exposed clifftop description correct
- Haytor and Hound Tor 7.8 km: distance and open moorland description correct
- Stanage Edge 9.2 km: distance and gritstone moorland description correct

No corrections required.

## Document 2: Methodology Page

**Overall assessment: PASS WITH CORRECTIONS**

Two source citation errors found: incorrect publication years for two PMC references. These are factual errors visible to readers and must be corrected before publication. One minor rounding note. One check cannot be verified without access to the scoring specification.

---

### Check 11 - VetCompass dataset size: "over 900,000 UK dogs"

**VERIFIED**

The research file records 905,543 UK dogs under primary veterinary care from PMC7303136. "Over 900,000" is accurate.

---

### Check 12 - French Bulldog HRI risk: "6.5 times the heat-related illness risk of a Labrador"

**VERIFIED WITH NOTE**

The figure is rounded from OR 6.49 (PMC7303136). "6.5 times" is an acceptable rounding.

Note for consistency: the French Bulldog guide cites the precise figure "6.49 times." The methodology page rounds to 6.5. Both are technically accurate. Editors may wish to standardise across both documents - precise (6.49) is preferable where a citation is present.

---

### Check 13 - English Bulldog HRI risk: "nearly 14 times the risk"

**VERIFIED**

The research file records the OR for English Bulldogs as 13.95. "Nearly 14 times" is accurate.

---

### Check 14 - Median heatstroke temperature: "16-17 degrees Celsius"

**VERIFIED**

The canine weather sensitivity research file records the VetCompass median temperature for heatstroke days in UK dogs as 16.5C (exertional) / 16.9C (all types). The range "16-17 degrees Celsius" accurately captures both figures. No correction required.

---

### Check 15 - Humidity: "above 80 percent, panting stops working effectively"

**VERIFIED**

Consistent with Drobatz and Macintire (PMC5800390), which is cited in the canine weather sensitivity research as the source for the 80% RH threshold at which panting effectiveness is substantially negated. The 1.6x multiplier above 80% is disclosed as an estimate, which is correct.

---

### Check 16 - Disclaimer accuracy against scoring specification

**CANNOT VERIFY - FLAG FOR EDITOR**

The disclaimer states scores are "based on published veterinary research and UK weather data" and are "intended as general guidance only." The disclaimer as written is appropriate standard language for a consumer-facing tool.

However, the disclaimer cannot be fully verified without access to the scoring specification document (docs/smart-weather-scoring-spec.md or equivalent). The scoring thresholds fact-check report (docs/reviews/scoring-thresholds-fact-check-april-2.md) found that the heat threshold of 22C used in the product is materially higher than the 16-17C median temperature at which VetCompass records heatstroke occurring. If the disclaimer claims the scores are "based on published veterinary research," the editor should confirm that the threshold choices are defensible against that claim.

This is not a factual error in the disclaimer text itself, but an open question for the editor and product owner to resolve before go-live.

---

### Check 17 - PMC source citations: titles, journals, and years

**TWO CORRECTIONS REQUIRED**

**Error 1 - PMC7303136 publication year:**

The methodology page lists this as: "Scientific Reports 2017"

The French Bulldog research file records this paper as: O'Neill et al., Scientific Reports, 2020 (PMC7303136)

**Correction required:** Change "Scientific Reports 2017" to "Scientific Reports 2020"

**Error 2 - PMC5800390 publication year:**

The methodology page lists this as: "Journal of Veterinary Emergency and Critical Care 2018"

The canine weather sensitivity research file records this paper (Drobatz and Macintire) as being from 1996. PMC5800390 is the PubMed Central record number, which does not correspond to a 1996 paper - papers of that era were not typically assigned PMC numbers. The research file should be treated as authoritative on the original publication date.

**Correction required:** Verify the correct year for PMC5800390 against PubMed directly. The "2018" date in the methodology appears to be incorrect. If this paper is the Drobatz and Macintire heatstroke physiology paper, the correct year is likely 1996 or the PMC number references a later republication. The citation needs to be confirmed and corrected before go-live.

**All other PMC links in the sources section** (PMC9144152, PMC7303136 URL) appear structurally correct. Year corrections above are the only factual errors found.
