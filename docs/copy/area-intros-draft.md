AREA: Hampshire
FILE: content/areas/hampshire.md
STATUS: NEW
COPY:
Farley Mount sits on the chalk ridge above Winchester with open off-lead woodland and an Iron Age hill fort at the summit. Dog walks in Hampshire also reach the Solent coast at Lepe - shingle beach, estuary views, and coastal walking that suits most dogs year-round. Ticks are active in Hampshire woodland from spring onwards; a lead and a tick check are both worth carrying.

FILE: content/areas/hertfordshire.md
STATUS: NEW
COPY:
Ashridge Estate in the Chiltern Hills is the anchor for dog walks in Hertfordshire - 2,000 hectares of ancient beech woodland, largely off-lead, managed by the National Trust. The bluebell display in late April and early May draws a lot of visitors; the rest of the year is quieter. Seasonal livestock zones mean the lead is needed in parts of the estate.

FILE: content/areas/edinburgh.md
STATUS: NEW
COPY:
Arthur's Seat rises to 251 metres from the centre of Edinburgh - an extinct volcanic hill with full off-lead access and views across the whole city. Dog walks in Edinburgh extend further than most expect: the Pentland Hills add reservoir walking and open moorland just a short drive south, taking you quickly out of the city and into open countryside.

AREA: Brecon Beacons
FILE: content/areas/brecon-beacons.md
STATUS: NEW
COPY:
Pen y Fan at 886 metres is the highest point in southern Britain, and the dog walks in Bannau Brycheiniog (Brecon Beacons) match the scale of the landscape around it. This is mountain walking country with mountain weather - routes change fast underfoot and in the air. Livestock graze year-round across open access land, so the lead is always close.

FILE: content/areas/snowdonia.md
STATUS: NEW
COPY:
Llyn Padarn at Llanberis is the gentlest entry point into dog walks in Eryri (Snowdonia) - a flat lakeside circuit with Yr Wyddfa's profile ahead for most of it. The national park's higher routes ask considerably more of both of you. Llyn Padarn is a good starting point and, on a clear day, gives you the full mountain atmosphere without the altitude.

FILE: content/areas/gower.md
STATUS: NEW
COPY:
Rhossili Bay is three miles of Atlantic beach at the western tip of the Gower Peninsula - dog-friendly all year, and one of the longer open beaches in Wales. Dog walks on the Gower extend from the beach to the Down ridge directly above it, and to Penllergare's Victorian woodland further east. The peninsula rewards a full day.
