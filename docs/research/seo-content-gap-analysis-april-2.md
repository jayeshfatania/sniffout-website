# SEO Content Gap Analysis - Sniffout
**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Researcher:** Sniffout Researcher

---

## Executive Summary

Sniffout operates in a high-volume UK search landscape dominated by generic walk directories and large charities, but none of these competitors have built dog-specific health, safety, or breed-sensitive content at meaningful depth. The breed sensitivity engine is the most differentiated asset Sniffout has, and it currently has no direct SEO expression - there are no breed-specific landing pages converting organic breed queries into app installs. The single biggest opportunity is a suite of breed exercise and walk guide pages targeting the UK's most popular breeds (Cockapoo, French Bulldog, Labrador, Spaniel, Shar Pei), anchored to the "walk smart for your breed" angle that only Sniffout can own. Alongside this, seasonal health content (particularly spring hazard coverage for April-May and summer heat guidance for June-July) is heavily searched and poorly served by walking apps. AllTrails has demonstrated that converting mobile SEO traffic into app installs via practical GPS value propositions is viable at scale - Sniffout should apply the same principle using walk pages and health guides as the conversion funnel.

Sniffout is not positioned to compete on Birmingham or Manchester without local walk data, but the Surrey and London coverage is already there in the app - it just lacks the index pages.

## 3. Content Format Opportunities

### Area Index Pages

Area index pages (e.g., "Dog walks in Surrey") represent the most immediately actionable content opportunity. Sniffout already has the underlying walk data to populate them - it is a matter of creating the index pages that aggregate existing content.

**Confirmed competitive ranking landscape:** For "dog walks in Surrey," the top results are Walkiees (walkiees.co.uk/dog-walks/surrey), Muddy Stilettos, Pets Corner, Pure Pet Food, Houndspot, and AllTrails. None of these tie breed sensitivity data to the location. Sniffout's area pages can differentiate on this basis.

**Priority area pages to create (based on Sniffout's existing walks):**
1. Dog walks in Surrey (aggregates: Richmond Park, Wimbledon Common, Box Hill, Leith Hill, Frensham Great Pond, Hindhead Common, Isabella Plantation)
2. Dog walks in London (aggregates: Richmond Park, Wimbledon Common, Hampstead Heath, Isabella Plantation)
3. Dog walks in the Lake District / Grasmere (aggregates Grasmere Lake, links to area)
4. Dog walks in Yorkshire (aggregates Stanage Edge, links to wider Peak District)
5. Dog walks in Sussex (aggregates Seven Sisters)
6. Dog walks in the New Forest (aggregates Burley Village)
7. Dog walks in North Yorkshire (aggregates Malham Cove)

Each page should include: aggregated walk cards with breed-suitability icons, health/seasonal hazard notes for the region, nearby dog-friendly places pulled from the app data.

### Breed Landing Pages

Breed landing pages structured as "[Breed] walking guide - how far, how often, which walks to choose" are an untapped format across all competitors. The breed sensitivity engine gives Sniffout both the content rationale and the product tie-in.

**Priority breed pages (based on UK popularity and search volume):**
1. Cockapoo walking guide (most popular UK crossbreed)
2. French Bulldog walking guide (second most popular pure breed, high search volume, heat sensitivity creates urgency)
3. Labrador walking guide (most registered pure breed, high volume)
4. Cocker Spaniel / Springer Spaniel walking guide
5. Cavapoo walking guide (fast-growing in popularity)
6. Border Collie walking guide (high exercise needs, large owner community)
7. Golden Retriever walking guide
8. Shar Pei walking guide (Sniffout already has Shar Pei health content - the walk guide is a natural extension)

Each breed page should include: recommended daily walk duration and distance, terrain preferences, weather limitations, and a section "Walks we recommend for [breed]" linking to appropriate Sniffout walk pages filtered for breed suitability.

### Seasonal Landing Pages

"Best autumn dog walks UK," "Best spring dog walks UK" etc. are searched at low-medium volume (estimated 500-2,500/month) but the seasonal nature means the content is perennially relevant and resurfaces each year. Petplan, Blue Cross, and lifestyle publishers rank for these terms - a walking app with breed filtering and health context would be a more useful result.

The key differentiator for Sniffout's seasonal pages is combining the walk recommendations with seasonal hazard guidance. "Best spring dog walks" becomes "Best spring dog walks - and what to watch out for (ticks, adders, grass seeds)" - a format no competitor is producing.

### "Can I Take My Dog To..." Pages

"Can I take my dog to [specific place]" is a natural-language query format that is currently answered by the venues themselves (National Trust, Forestry England) or generic guides. Sniffout's walk pages can serve this intent when the location is a Sniffout destination:

- "Can I take my dog to Richmond Park?" (dogs allowed, deer management seasonal restrictions)
- "Can I take my dog to Hampstead Heath?" (dogs allowed, designated off-lead areas)
- "Can I take my dog to the Seven Sisters?" (allowed, coastal path rules)

These pages would not be separate from existing walk pages - they would be addressed within each walk page's content, targeting the natural-language query with an FAQ section or structured content block.

### "Dog Walks with Pub" Format

This is a high-engagement format (the "holy trinity of British weekends" per search result data) and Sniffout's Nearby tab data is genuinely differentiating here. A series of "Dog walks with pub near [location]" pages would combine walk data with nearby pub data in a format that no walking app currently produces as structured content.

## 4. Website-to-App Conversion Recommendations

### What AllTrails Does

AllTrails' CEO has directly stated that converting "mobile-first SEO traffic into incremental organic app installs" is "a huge driver" of the business. Their approach:
- Walk pages provide GPS route data
- The practical value of GPS navigation incentivises app download without a hard sell
- No explicit sales pitch - the utility does the work
- The install converts users into content creators who generate more UGC, feeding the SEO growth loop

AllTrails' walk pages on mobile show app install prompts tied to specific feature value (route navigation, offline maps). The user has a reason to install rooted in the specific walk they just found.

### What Go Jauntly Does

Go Jauntly uses three distinct CTA placements (confirmed via site fetch):
- Fixed announcement bar at top: "Discover nature-filled routes today! [Download Our App]"
- Navigation header button: "Get the app" (yellow background, always visible)
- Hero section: App Store and Google Play badges with direct links

Their CTAs are generic ("Download Our App") and not tied to walk-specific utility.

### Smart App Banner Data

Published data from Branch's smart banner research:
- Banner-referred app users show 2.6x higher in-app purchase rate than general app users
- Users entering from banners drive 8% of app sessions but generate 20% of app purchase revenue
- Showing an install prompt after 2+ page visits increases installation rate by over 30%
- PWA install prompts outperform native app download prompts: Lyft data shows users click "Install PWA" 40% more often than "Download App"
- Rakuten 24 achieved 450% increase in visitor retention within one month of implementing a custom install prompt

### PWA-Specific Conversion Data

PWA install prompt data from documented case studies:
- JD.ID: overall mobile conversion rate increased 53%, with installed users converting at 200% higher rates
- Starbucks: daily active users doubled after PWA launch
- Kaporal: 60% fewer bounces, 15% more conversions

The Lyft data point is directly relevant to Sniffout as a PWA: users are more likely to accept a PWA install prompt than an "open in App Store" prompt. This is a structural advantage for Sniffout over AllTrails and Komoot which direct users to native app stores.

### Recommended CTA Strategy for Sniffout

**Principle:** Tie the install CTA to breed-specific utility, not generic discovery. The unique value Sniffout provides is breed-filtered intelligence - this should be the hook.

**Recommended placements and copy:**

1. Walk page top banner (persistent, dismissible):
"This walk is rated for [breed] - see more walks for your dog in the app"
This is the single most compelling CTA Sniffout can use and no competitor can replicate it.

2. In-content prompt after health/safety section:
"Get live weather safety alerts for this walk on the Sniffout app"
Converts users reading hazard content into utility-motivated installs.

3. After area index page scroll (bottom of page):
"See all walks near you, filtered for your dog's breed - install the Sniffout app"

4. Sticky bottom bar on mobile (appearing after 10 seconds or 50% page scroll):
"Open in Sniffout - see if this walk suits your breed"

5. Breed page CTA:
"Find walks near you rated for [breed] - add Sniffout to your home screen"
This capitalises on the PWA install advantage (add to home screen framing converts better than "download").

**Conversion rate expectations (realistic estimates for a new/growing PWA):**
- Web-to-PWA install from walk pages: 1-3% of mobile visitors (based on PWA benchmark data, noting Sniffout is not yet a large established brand)
- Web-to-install from breed pages: 3-6% (higher intent, more differentiated value proposition)
- Web-to-install from health/safety pages: 1-2% (lower commercial intent, primarily informational)

These are conservative benchmarks. As brand recognition grows and the app catalogue expands, conversion rates should improve toward the upper end of industry data (Rakuten 24's 450% retention improvement represents a ceiling at full optimisation, not a starting expectation for a growing app).

## 6. Content Gap Summary - Priority Order

The following content pieces represent the highest-return opportunities given Sniffout's current coverage, the breed sensitivity engine, and the April 2026 timing of this analysis. Items are ordered by expected impact on traffic and installs.

**1. Dog walks in Surrey - area index page**
Rationale: Six of Sniffout's 14 walks are in Surrey or directly adjacent. This page aggregates existing content into a format that captures 2,000-4,000 monthly searches. It has the lowest production effort of any item on this list (curation of existing walks + brief area intro) and the highest traffic potential for the near term. Should include a breed-suitability legend and seasonal hazard notes for the Surrey Hills.

**2. Dog walks in London - area index page**
Rationale: Four walks (Richmond Park, Wimbledon Common, Hampstead Heath, Isabella Plantation) qualify. "Dog walks near London" estimated at 3,000-5,000 monthly searches. London audience is Sniffout's core market given the app's walk coverage.

**3. French Bulldog walking guide**
Rationale: French Bulldogs are the second most popular pure breed in the UK (42,000+ KC registrations), estimated 2,000-4,000 monthly searches for exercise/walk queries, and their brachycephalic sensitivity is exactly what the breed engine is designed to address. Sniffout already has brachycephalic content to draw from. This page writes itself - it is the purest expression of the breed sensitivity engine as SEO content.

**4. Cockapoo walking guide**
Rationale: Cockapoos are the most popular crossbreed in the UK and possibly the single most common breed owned. "How far can a Cockapoo walk" and related terms get 1,500-3,000 estimated searches/month. Competition for Cockapoo-specific walk guidance is very low. This guide creates a large addressable audience with no content to displace.

**5. Adder bite dog guide - with seasonal walk notes**
Rationale: It is April 2026. Adder season is now. The peak search period is April-July. Sniffout has no content here, and the geographic overlap with existing walks (Seven Sisters, Hindhead Common, Leith Hill) gives the guide unique contextual value. This is the single most time-sensitive piece on the list.

**6. How far should I walk my dog - breed-by-breed guide**
Rationale: Estimated 5,000-10,000 monthly searches, medium competition, and the content is a direct expression of the breed engine. A page structured as "How far should I walk my [breed]" with a breed selector UI element would be an ideal interactive content piece driving both SEO and app engagement.

**7. Is it too hot to walk my dog - temperature guide with breed sensitivity layer**
Rationale: Summer is 8-10 weeks away. This is a high-volume seasonal query (3,000-6,000/month peak) that Sniffout partially covers but has not built a dedicated optimised page for. Adding a breed sensitivity layer ("brachycephalic breeds at risk above 18C" vs "healthy adult Labs at risk above 25C") would be uniquely Sniffout.

**8. Labrador walking guide**
Rationale: Most registered pure breed in UK, high search volume, and Labradors are a well-served niche in pet content generally - which means this page needs to differentiate on the walk-specific angle (which routes suit Labs, what distance is right by age, recall and livestock safety). Sniffout's existing walks with open space and off-lead sections are ideal Lab recommendations.

**9. Dog walks in the New Forest - area index page**
Rationale: Burley Village is Sniffout's only New Forest walk. The New Forest is one of the most searched dog walking regions in the UK (it is a national park and major tourist destination). An area page acknowledging the wider New Forest context while featuring Burley Village would capture "dog walks New Forest" searches which compete in the 2,000-4,000 range.

**10. Dog walks with pub - Surrey/London pages**
Rationale: The Nearby tab pub data is a unique asset. "Dog walks with pub Surrey" is an estimated 1,000-2,500 monthly query and is dominated by editorial content (Muddy Stilettos, OS GetOutside) without the walk data specificity Sniffout can provide. Combining route data with verified pub data is a genuinely differentiated format.

**11. Spaniel walking guide (Cocker and Springer combined)**
Rationale: Spaniels are among the most popular UK breeds and have specific exercise needs (high energy, scenting drive, recall challenges). An estimated 1,000-2,000 monthly searches for spaniel exercise/walk guidance with low-medium competition.

**12. Can I walk my dog in the heat / snow / rain - weather condition pages**
Rationale: A series of three short pages targeting the "can I walk my dog in [condition]" queries. Each is estimated at 1,500-4,000 monthly searches and all are gaps in Sniffout's current content. These could be produced quickly and scheduled for the appropriate seasonal window.

**13. Cavapoo walking guide**
Rationale: Rapidly growing in popularity, very low competition for walk-specific content. Estimated 500-1,000 monthly searches, a number that will increase as ownership grows.

**14. Dog walks in Yorkshire - area index page**
Rationale: Stanage Edge exists in the catalogue. Yorkshire dog walks is an estimated 2,000-3,500 monthly search term. The existing walk is a strong anchor for a broader Peak District/Yorkshire Dales context page.

**15. Alabama rot - seasonal update for 2026**
Rationale: Sniffout has existing Alabama rot content. Given the disease is highly seasonal (November-May) and 2025-2026 case numbers are in active tracking (334 confirmed UK cases with 6 in 2025 as of recent data), a seasonal update post refreshes existing content and recaptures search traffic during the active season.
