## Richmond Park Deer Loop
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- ticks-dogs-uk
- heatstroke-dogs-hot-weather-walking
- blue-green-algae-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Richmond Park's large deer population makes it one of the highest-risk tick environments in London, justifying ticks regardless of season; Pen Ponds are still freshwater with documented algae risk in warm weather, making the blue-green algae guide safety-relevant; the 7.2km exposed parkland route with no shade is a genuine heatstroke risk in summer. Alabama rot excluded because the terrain is short-cropped grassland rather than muddy woodland. Grass seeds excluded because the deer park is managed and does not have the long heathland or meadow grass where seeds cause problems. Cattle excluded because deer are wildlife, not livestock.

## Isabella Plantation
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- ticks-dogs-uk
- alabama-rot-dogs-uk
- blue-green-algae-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** The plantation is dense, shaded, damp woodland with leaf litter - classic tick habitat; the streams and ponds create still water conditions relevant to blue-green algae in warm months; the woodland character with damp mulched ground and water features mirrors the muddy woodland conditions associated with Alabama rot, making it the most relevant guide for the Nov-May season on this walk. Heatstroke excluded because the route is short (1.4km), shaded, and on-lead. Grass seeds excluded because this is a maintained woodland garden, not heathland or meadow.

## Leith Hill and Coldharbour
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- ticks-dogs-uk
- alabama-rot-dogs-uk
- cattle-dog-walkers-uk

**Reasoning:** Mixed woodland and heath is prime tick habitat throughout the season; the route gets muddy in winter and the woodland sections are exactly the environment associated with Alabama rot, particularly Nov-May; livestock are possible near Coldharbour village, making the cattle guide directly relevant for the section through or near the village. Heatstroke excluded because the woodland provides shade and the walk does not have the sustained exposure of moorland routes. Grass seeds applicable to the heath sections in summer but displaced by three stronger assignments.

## Frensham Great Pond
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- blue-green-algae-dogs-uk
- grass-seeds-dogs-uk
- ticks-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Dogs actively swim in the pond, making blue-green algae the most safety-critical assignment on this walk - warm still water with high visitor pressure is a textbook bloom environment; sandy heathland with heather and long grass is one of the primary grass seed environments in summer; heathland and rough grassland around the pond are also high-risk tick habitat, particularly with deer using the heathland. Alabama rot excluded because the terrain is dry sandy heath rather than muddy woodland. Heatstroke relevant but the swimming opportunity and shorter distance (4.2km) make it lower priority than the three assigned.

## Hindhead Common and Devils Punch Bowl

- ticks-dogs-uk
- grass-seeds-dogs-uk

- grass-seeds-dogs-uk

## Box Hill Summit Loop
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- ticks-dogs-uk
- heatstroke-dogs-hot-weather-walking
- grass-seeds-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Chalk scrub, woodland edge, and grassland are all tick-prone environments; the open exposed downland and the exertion of the summit climb make heatstroke relevant in summer - chalk downland offers minimal shade; chalk downland and scrub margins have the long grasses and seed heads typical of grass seed injury in summer. Alabama rot excluded because chalk drains well and the terrain is not characteristic muddy woodland. Blue-green algae excluded because there is no significant standing water on the route.

## Seven Sisters Cliffs
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- heatstroke-dogs-hot-weather-walking
- ticks-dogs-uk
- blue-green-algae-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** The 8.4km clifftop route is the most exposed walk in the list with no shade and strong reflected light from the chalk - heatstroke is the most relevant guide here in summer; coastal grassland and scrub carry ticks, and the Cuckmere Haven area with its rough vegetation is particularly relevant; the Cuckmere River has slow-moving freshwater sections upstream of the haven where blue-green algae blooms can develop in warm settled weather, and dogs drink and swim there. Alabama rot excluded because this is exposed chalk downland and coastal grassland, not muddy woodland. Grass seeds less relevant than on heathland or moorland - coastal turf is short and managed.

## Haytor and Hound Tor
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- ticks-dogs-uk
- grass-seeds-dogs-uk
- alabama-rot-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Open moorland with heather and rough grass is prime tick habitat; Dartmoor moorland in summer is one of the most significant grass seed environments in England, with dogs regularly presenting with grass seed injuries after moorland walks; the boggy ground after rain and the South West location - where Alabama rot cases have historically been most concentrated - make this a genuine Alabama rot route particularly in winter and spring. Heatstroke relevant for the 7.8km exposed route in summer but displaced by three stronger assignments. Dartmoor ponies are free-roaming wildlife, not livestock in fields with footpaths, so the cattle guide does not apply.

## Stanage Edge and Hathersage Moor
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- ticks-dogs-uk
- heatstroke-dogs-hot-weather-walking
- grass-seeds-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Moorland heather is high-risk tick habitat; at 9.2km this is the longest walk in the list, fully exposed on a gritstone edge with no shade - heatstroke is a real risk in warm weather and the exertion of moorland walking increases it; rough moorland and heather carry grass seeds that become a hazard in summer. Alabama rot relevant to Peak District moorland in principle, but the gritstone and sandy peat terrain drains better than the lowland clay woodland where most confirmed cases occur - ticks and heatstroke are higher-priority assignments for this specific environment.

## Hampstead Heath and Ponds
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- blue-green-algae-dogs-uk
- ticks-dogs-uk
- alabama-rot-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Dogs actively swim in the heath ponds, making blue-green algae safety-critical - warm still ponds in a urban setting with high nutrient levels are at elevated bloom risk in summer; mixed woodland with damp leaf litter is high-risk tick habitat; the woodland sections of the heath with shaded paths and damp ground mirror the conditions associated with Alabama rot, particularly in wetter months. Heatstroke relevant in summer but the ponds provide cooling and the terrain is mixed rather than fully exposed - lower priority than the three assigned.

## Malham Cove and Gordale Scar
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- cattle-dog-walkers-uk
- ticks-dogs-uk
- alabama-rot-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** The walk has livestock throughout, including fields that the footpath crosses and the enclosed dale sections near Gordale, making the cattle guide directly relevant; limestone grassland and rough valley vegetation carry ticks and Yorkshire Dales livestock farms increase deer and sheep tick pressure; the stream crossings and damp valley floor with wet grass and boggy sections after rain create conditions relevant to Alabama rot, and the Yorkshire Dales has confirmed cases. Heatstroke less relevant - limestone pavement is exposed but the walk is moderate distance and the valley sections provide some shelter. Grass seeds less relevant than on open heathland or moorland.

## Burley Village and Forest Walks
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- cattle-dog-walkers-uk
- alabama-rot-dogs-uk
- ticks-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Free-roaming cattle are present throughout the New Forest and dogs must be kept under control near them - this is the walk on the list where the cattle guide is most directly applicable as encounters are near-certain rather than possible; the New Forest is the site of the first confirmed UK Alabama rot cases and historically has the highest concentration of confirmed CRGV cases - ancient woodland with muddy paths in winter and spring makes this the highest-priority Alabama rot walk in the list; ancient woodland is prime tick habitat with deer, cattle, and ponies all increasing tick pressure. Heatstroke excluded because the woodland provides shade for most of the 4.8km route.

## Grasmere Lake Circuit
**Assigned guides (max 3):**
- blue-green-algae-dogs-uk
- cattle-dog-walkers-uk
- ticks-dogs-uk

**Reasoning:** Grasmere lake is a slow-moving freshwater body and the circuit includes lakeside sections where dogs drink and wade - blue-green algae is a genuine warm-weather risk and worth knowing about before a swimming stop; livestock are present near the village and the lakeside meadows, with the route passing alongside grazing fields on the circuit; lakeside meadow and long rough grass throughout the circuit is tick habitat with high deer and sheep pressure typical of the Lake District. Alabama rot relevant to Lake District woodland but the majority of this route is lakeside and meadow rather than muddy woodland, making ticks and cattle higher-priority assignments.
