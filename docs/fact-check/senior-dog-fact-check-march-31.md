## Flags (not automatic fails - Tom's attention required before publication)

### FLAG 1 - "Cold stress" vs "hypothermia" (line 80)

The research says monitoring for "hypothermia signs." The article says monitoring for "signs of cold stress." The signs listed are identical in both cases (shivering, whining, pulling paws back, tucking the tail, reluctance to go outside). Cold stress and hypothermia are related but technically distinct: cold stress precedes hypothermia.

"Cold stress" is arguably more accurate given the symptoms listed (these are early warning signs, not signs of established hypothermia). However, the change from the source language should be noted.

Recommended action: No change needed. "Cold stress" is the more accurate term for the listed signs. Tom's framing is defensible.

---

### FLAG 2 - PDSA rest stop quote partially paraphrased (line 92)

Article: "PDSA's guidance is direct: if a dog lies down on a walk, they need to rest."

Research verbatim PDSA: "If they stop chasing a toy or lie down on a walk, they need to rest."

Tom has removed "stop chasing a toy" from the paraphrase. The article does not present this as a direct quote, so this is a paraphrase rather than a quotation error. The meaning is preserved. However, the full PDSA guidance also covers toy-chasing stops, not only lying down.

Recommended action: No change required. The paraphrase is accurate for the context (the article is about walks, not toy-chasing). If Tom wishes to be more complete, the full sentence could be restored: "If [dog name] stops or lies down on a walk, they need to rest" - which covers both cases without the toy reference.
