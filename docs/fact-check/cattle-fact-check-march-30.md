## Flag items

### FLAG-01: HSE source -- URL returns 404; claim phrasing not confirmed in accessible text

**Article text:** "Cattle are responsible for more deaths on UK footpaths than any other animal. That is an HSE finding that is frequently cited without the context that makes it useful: most incidents involve dogs."

**Source issue:** The HSE cattle guidance document AIS17.htm referenced during research returns a 404. The live document for England and Wales is AIS17EW (available on the HSE website). The article does not cite the URL directly, so this does not create an in-article broken link, but the sourcing for the claim needs to be verifiable.

**Claim verification:** The accessible HSE statistics on cattle fatalities support that cattle are the most dangerous farmyard animal for members of the public. The specific phrasing "more deaths on UK footpaths than any other animal" was not confirmed in the text of the accessible HSE guidance reviewed. The claim is widely cited by UK countryside safety bodies (RoSPA, NFU, Countryside Alliance) and is consistent with HSE data, but the exact phrasing as an "HSE finding" requires a citable source.

**Fix:** Either cite the specific HSE document (AIS17EW for England/Wales) or soften to "HSE statistics on cattle fatalities show cattle are the most dangerous farmyard animal for members of the public." Remove "on UK footpaths" if this specific qualifier cannot be confirmed.

### FLAG-02: Beef bull restriction -- "in the company of cows or heifers"

**Article text:** "Beef breed bulls can be, provided they are in the company of cows or heifers."

**Legal position:** Under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981, dairy breed bulls (or bulls of a recognised dairy breed) over 10 months old cannot be kept in a field through which a public right of way passes. Beef breed bulls may be kept in such fields provided they are accompanied by cows or heifers. Tom's statement is accurate.

**Note:** No fix required. Included for verification completeness.

### FLAG-03: "Let the dog go" -- confirmed

**Article text:** "If they continue to approach and get very close, and you feel you are in genuine danger: let the lead go."

**Sources:** HSE guidance explicitly advises releasing the dog if cattle are charging. The advice is confirmed and accurate.

**Note:** No fix required.

### FLAG-04: Calving season window -- "March to July"

**Article text:** "In spring and early summer, when calves are young and mothers are most protective..." and "Before any walk that might cross agricultural land between March and July..."

**Sources:** UK calving seasons vary by farming system. Spring calving (February-April) is common for suckler herds. Autumn calving is also practiced. The article's "March to July" framing captures the most common spring calving window plus the period when calves remain young. Blue Cross and NFU guidance align with a spring caution period. The framing is appropriate as a practical heuristic.

**Note:** No fix required. Flagged for completeness.

**Note:** Acceptable as established safety guidance. No fix required.
